Kabogo dares Uhuru to state publicly if he supports DP Ruto or not

February 15, 2024
Post

- Kabogo said Uhuru has never informed him he is against Ruto's State House bid

- The politician asked the president to clear the air on the topic while it is still early

- The ex-governor urged Ruto to continue winning hearts of voters for him to be able to beat competition

Former Kiambu Governor William Kabogo has asked President Uhuru Kenyatta to publicly declare his stand over William Ruto's bid to succeed him.

Kabogo was speaking at a church service in Molo, Nakuru county where he pledged massive support for Ruto when the 2022 General Election will take place.

His remarks surprised many as the former governor has been known to be a big critique of the deputy president.

"There is no day I have had a conversation with the president and he mentioned to me that he does not support Ruto. However, if he wants to say, I challenge him to make the remarks whilst it is still early. For how long shall he wait?" posed Kabogo.

According to him, it was high time Ruto focused on winning the hearts of voters.

He advised the DP to forget about the political debt his allies have been talking about that Uhuru's backyards need to pay for Ruto's support during the 2013 and 2017 presidential elections.

"Do not just sit down and assume the promise will give you the seat. Go out there and look for it. Kenyans are the one who will elect the person they want to lead them. The only reason that they will have to rally behind you is by ensuring they are living a descent life," the ex-governor stated.
