Ken Okoth: Late MP's mother made frantic attempts to stop cremation

June 27, 2024
Post

- Angeline Ajwang' wanted the body of her son to be flown home in Homa Bay for burial

- Okoth's wife Monica insisted on having the body cremated saying it was his wish

- The MP was cremated on Saturday morning at Keriokor crematorium in absence of the mother

It has now emerged Angeline Ajwang', mother of late Kibra MP Ken Okoth, put up a spirited fight against son's cremation but lost the battle to Okoth's wife Monica.

The cremation took place on Saturday, August 3, morning in absence of the mother who reportedly tried everything she possibly could to prevent son's body from being burnt, including calling Luo MPs to help her stop Monica from going ahead with the cremation plan, all in vain.

A report by The Standard on Monday, August 5, revealed a grief-stricken Ajwang' reached out to MPs from Migori and Kisumu counties hoping they could intervene and fly Okoth's body home for burial in line with the Luo traditions.

“My kinsmen, I just received a message from Mama Ken (Angeline Ajwang') and I called her back. She is pleading for help so that she can bury her son..." one of the Luo legislators shared with colleagues in a WhatsApp group.

Unfortunately for the bereaved mother, there was little the Luo politicians could do to help, especially considering the issue at hand was a family matter.

Having objected to the cremation which was shrouded in confusion and secrecy, Ajwang' and other family members were not present during the ceremony held at around 3am on Saturday at Keriokor crematorium in Nairobi.

According to Okoth's nephew Elvis Oluoch, the mother was not even aware her son was going to be burnt to ashes at Keriokor that Saturday morning and only came to learn about it after it was already done.

Although the widow, Monica, maintained that was how her husband wanted her body to be disposed, a defiant mother-in-law and other family members couldn't hear any of it and continued to make plans for Okoth's burial back at his maternal home in Homa Bay county.

"We only heard Ken left a Will directing he be cremated upon death. None of our family members has set eyes on that said Will. All through, we have been making arrangements to have Ken transported to Homa Bay and be buried on his mother’s land. We did not know there were other plans," said a disappointed Oluoch.

Besides Okoth's mother and some of the other relatives, Luo council of elders was also angered by the way the late MP's body was disposed saying it was against the Luo culture.

The council's chairman Nyandiko Ongadi on Sunday asked Monica to avail herself for rituals to be conducted in Kabondo, Homa Bay county.

Part of the rituals include inheriting Monica as per the long-standing Luo tradition which dictates the widow can be remarried by a brother of her late husband.

Ongadi argued the rituals must be conducted because Okoth was neither brought home nor buried in line with the Luo traditions.

He explained when the body of the deceased is not buried in the grave, then a banana stem is usually buried in place of the body and that family members must be present.
