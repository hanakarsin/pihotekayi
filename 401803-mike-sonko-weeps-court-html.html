Mike Sonko weeps in court, claims prosecution frustrating him

June 03, 2024
Post

- Mike Sonko accused the police of adding a count of assault on his charge sheet after he refused to withdraw an affidavit he filed at the High Court

- According to the ex-governor, the affidavit adversely mentions President Uhuru's sister Christine Pratt Kenyatta

- Sonko, who has been a guest of the state for at least two days now, is facing a graft case where he is accused of having a hand in loss of about KSh 14 million county funds

Former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko was on Wednesday, February 3, reduced to tears when he was arraigned before an anti-corruption court in Nairobi.

Sonko, who appeared before Chief Magistrate Douglas Ogoti for mentioning of a graft case he is facing, broke down while trying to explain to the magistrate how the prosecution was frustrating him.

The ex-Nairobi county chief, who stood humbly before the court while clad in a red jacket and a white t-shirt, spent the better part of the time he was given to address the court touching his mask, perhaps draining tears that were flowing from his eyes.

At some point, Ogoti even asked him to relax and "not to cry" as he spoke since he was jotting down his concerns.

Realising that both the judge and the other court attendants were concerned about his moods, he told the court he is "always emotional" but this time around it was difficult for him to fight back his tears.

He claimed the police had slapped him with more charges after he refused to withdraw an affidavit that adversely mentions President Uhuru Kenyatta's sister Christine Pratt Kenyatta.

"The officers asked me to withdraw an affidavit I had filed at the high court against the president's sister. When I refused, they pressed against me assault charges. I think this is very important for the court to note," Sonko said as he sunk back on the wooden bench where his lawyers were sat pensively.

Sonko also expressed frustrations over the move by the court to deny him bail after he was arraigned on Tuesday and charged with several counts among them assault and destruction of property.

On Monday, the politician was summoned to the DCI to shed light on the explosive "confession" he made about two weeks ago that Interior PS Karanja Kibicho was among "deep state agents" who had a hand in ODM chaos witnessed after the 2017 General Election. He dared Kibicho to sue him.

Sonko's legal team lead by Senior Counsel John Khaminwa urged the court to grant them 30 days to organise themselves.
