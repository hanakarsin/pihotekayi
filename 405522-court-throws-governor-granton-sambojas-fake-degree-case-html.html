Taita Taveta governor Granton Samboja has received reprieve after the High Court in Nairobi dismissed a case challenging his academic qualification.

The Ethics and Anti-Corruption Commission (EACC) had moved to the court seeking to have the governor declared unqualified and ineligible to run for gubernatorial seat.

The ethics agency claimed Samboja presented a fake degree certificate to the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission to be cleared to vie for the seat in 2017.

However, Justice James Makau poked holes in the EACC petition which he noted had bypassed other avenues such as the election court.

Makau said although the petitioner raised fundamental questions in regards to the authenticity of Samboja's university certificate, he observed the High Court had no jurisdiction to hear it.

"This court has no jurisdiction to hear and entertain the petition. Though there are serious questions of qualification, he was not charged with any offence of falsifying documents. Documents were not interrogated by IEBC,” he ruled.

In 2017, Kenyatta University dealt the county boss a major blow when it told a court in Nairobi that the governor was not one of its students as he had alleged.

According to Samboja's certificate, he graduated from Kenyatta University in 2009 with a Bachelor of Commerce degree.

A voter from Voi sued the governor on grounds that his academic credentials were questionable and did not meet the requirements for the governorship seat.
