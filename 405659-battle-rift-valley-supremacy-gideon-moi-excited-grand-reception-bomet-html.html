Battle for Rift Valley supremacy: Gideon Moi excited after grand reception in Bomet

April 30, 2024
Post

- Gideon Moi is on a mission to fend off Deputy President William Ruto influence in Rift Valley

- He has traversed several counties in the region among them Kericho, Bomet, West Pokot, Turkana, Nakuru and Kajiado to popularise his presidential bid

- On the hand, Ruto has been focusing on selling his agenda outside Rift Valley and appears to be keen on establishing himself as a national leader

Baringo Senator Gideon Moi has been carrying out a series of political rallies in Rift Valley in a bid to ward off the influence of Deputy President William Ruto ahead of the 2022 General Election.

On Friday, March 12, Gideon toured Bomet county where he pulled a huge crowd despite running into headwinds with a group of youths supporting Ruto.

Wooing Bomet

Bomet is the home county of KANU secretary general Nick Salat, a man Gideon is banking on to popularise his State House bid.

During the tour, the Baringo senator opened a KANU office at Mulot Sunset to strengthen the party's grassroots network. He later addressed a gathering at CDF grounds in Longisa and held a fundraiser for the construction of classrooms for Kogunoi Primary School.

Through his Twitter handle, the young Moi thanked the people of Bomet county for the warm reception they accorded him.

Isacc Ruto factor

However, Gideon has an uphill task to penetrate Bomet county considering the presence of former governor Isaac Ruto who recently joined Ruto's bandwagon.

The Chama Cha Mashinani (CCM) leader holds sway in the county and may fend off KANU's rising popularity in due course.

Incumbent Governor Hillary Barchok and Senator Christopher Langat are also allies of the deputy president.

Moi's visit to Bomet came barely came days after held other rallies in Baringo, Turkana and Garissa counties where he popularised the BBI bill and his 2022 presidential ambitions.

The KANU leader has formed an alliance with Musalia Mudavadi (ANC), Kalonzo Musyoka (Wiper) and Moses Wetangu'la (FORD Kenya).

During the Matungu and Kabuchai by-elections, Gideon assured his partners in the newly formed coalition that he would take charge of Rift Valley politics before 2022.

"Don't worry about Rift Valley, leave it to me. I will strengthen things," he said.

Succession matrix

The young Moi is part of a team of national leaders supporting President Uhuru Kenyatta and Opposition leader Raila Odinga's handshake and the push for constitutional reforms through the BBI bill.

He sits well in Uhuru's succession matrix, considering their existing strong family ties and cordial relationship at a personal level.

The Baringo senator is also likely to benefit from the apparent fall out between Uhuru and Ruto, who have been political buddies for a long time.

He is one of the politicians who stand a chance of being endorsed by the president as a compromised candidate.

Ruto's fighting chance

On the hand, Ruto is making strides in popularising his presidential bid by courting new allies across the country.

The DP, using his hustler tag, has been traversing different parts of the country to woo the youth, the unemployed and less privileged Kenyans to support his course.

He is currently considered by a section of political pundits as a front runner in the race to State House.
