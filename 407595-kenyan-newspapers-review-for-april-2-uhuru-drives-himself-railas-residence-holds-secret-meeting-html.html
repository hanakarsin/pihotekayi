On Friday, April 2, the Kenyan newspapers reported widely on the secret meeting between President Uhuru Kenyatta and former prime minister Raila Odinga at the latter's residence in Karen.

The papers also noted there were fears of cheating during the ongoing Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examinations even as Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha assured the country of credibility in the tests.

1. The Standard

President Uhuru Kenyatta drove himself to Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) leader Raila Odinga's home in Karen on Wednesday, March 31, where the duo reportedly held a secret meeting for two hours.

Their talks focussed on reviving the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) and supporting the fight against the COVID-19 pandemic.

"The president and the former premier discussed his health, the status of the handshake and the anxiety created by the partial lockdown to contain COVID-19, which has angered some Kenyans," a source intimidated to the publication.

After the meeting, it was reported the two leaders agreed to appear in public together to calm down their support bases and assure Raila's foot soldiers who have been jittery that the BBI process is on course.

2. Daily Nation

Some test papers suspected to be part of the ongoing KCSE exams are being sold on social media, raising nationwide cheating fears.

The papers were being circulated on social media platforms such as WhatsApp and Telegram, where students and teachers could easily access them.

It was reported some rogue examiners could be opening the tests before the scheduled time and trading them for KSh 1,000 for each paper.

However, Education CS George Magoha dismissed the papers being sold as fake and warned parents and students against falling into the trap.

"We have seen an emergence of fake papers being circulated in some areas of the country, purported to be genuine exam materials. Our examination has not been leaked and won't be leaked," the CS assured.

3. People Daily

Former United States president Barack Obama is set to visit the country next week to pay his last respect to his late grandmother, Mama Sarah Obama.

The 44th US president would be accompanied by members of his family and his security personnel.

Mama Sarah rested on March 29 at the age of 99 and was laid to rest on March 30, according to simple Islamic traditions.

Obama's visit to her grandmother's grave in the sleepy Kogelo village in Siaya county would be expected to link him with his ancestors as per the Luo traditions.

4. Taifa Leo

The ODM party has dismissed speculations the outfit would enter into an alliance with Deputy President William Ruto ahead of the 2022 General Election.

Ugunja Member of Parliament Opiyo Wandayi, who doubles up as ODM's director of political affairs, said the Opposition party would continue championing for the implementation of BBI and supporting the handshake between Uhuru and Raila.
