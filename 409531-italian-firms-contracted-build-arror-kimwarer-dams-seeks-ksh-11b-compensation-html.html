Italian Firms Contracted to Build Arror, Kimwarer Dams Seek KSh 11b in Compensation

June 26, 2024
Post

- The three Italian firms have moved to court seeking more than KSh 11 billion over the annulment of the projects

- Elgeyo Marakwet Senator Kipchumba Murkomen has accused the State of allegedly denying locals such projects in the name of fighting corruption

- The two projects were expected to create an estimated 2,000 job opportunities and turn around the Kerio Valley economy

The row surrounding the Arror and Kimwarer Dams scandal has taken a fresh twist after Italian companies contracted to implement the projects sued Kenya for the cancellation of the deals.

The firms; Cooperativa Muratori & Cementisti -CMC Di Ravenna Societa Cooperativa (Italy), Itinera S.P.A and CMC Di Ravanna- Itinera JV S.C.P.A have jointly moved to court seeking more than KSh 11 billion over the annulment of the projects.

Elgeyo Marakwet senator Kipchumba Murkomen has, however, accused the State of allegedly denying locals such projects in the name of fighting corruption.

Murkomen said the two were part of the Vision 2030 flagship projects.

“I said it from the word go that the main objective of the cancellation of these projects was to deny the good people of Elgeyo Marakwet the projects. It is now clear that this had nothing to do with fighting corruption. In the end, the Kenyan government will be compelled to pay. Unfortunately, state sadists would rather pay the contractor for doing nothing than to see the projects in my county,” said Murkomen.

Arror and Kimwarer multi-purpose dams were to be constructed at an inflated cost of KSh 63 billion, with the former costing KSh 35 billion and the latter KSh 28 billion.

According to court papers, the Italian firms filed the case against the Kerio Valley Development Authority (KVDA) on December 15, 2020, asking for KSh 12 billion.

This was exclusive of the costs to be incurred to pay the lawyers involved during the hearing.

The companies also want the court to declare that the cancellation of the contracts is irregular and unlawful.

It further wants KVDA to be held accountable and take full responsibility for the delays in the construction and completion of the two multi-billion projects.

“Accordingly, order KVDA to pay the petitioners all the amounts conditionally indicated as US$114,177,645, or any other different sum that will result due to claimants in the course of the proceedings, plus interest as applicable,” the court papers read.

Solicitor-General Kennedy Ogeto has since written a letter to Elgeyo Marakwet governor Alex Tolgos, requesting that he be furnished with all certification and communication regarding the dam projects.

At the same time, Ogeto has appointed senior state counsel Charles Wamwayi and Sheila Mammet to liaise with Tolgos’s office as the government puts in place mechanisms of defending the case filed by the Italians.

Former National Treasury Cabinet Secretary Henry Rotich is among several senior Treasury and KVDA officials who have so far been charged with economic crimes related to the construction of the two dams.

TUKO.co.ke understands that the accused face 24 charges, among them abuse of office and conspiracy to defraud.

The two projects were expected to create an estimated 2,000 job opportunities and turn around Kerio Valley economy.
