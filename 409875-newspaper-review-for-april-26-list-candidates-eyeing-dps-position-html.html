Newspapers Review For April 26: List of Candidates Eyeing Deputy President Position

August 15, 2024
Post

On Monday, April 26, the newspapers extensively reported on the hopefuls eyeing the Deputy President's position in the 2022 General Election.

The dailies also touched on the state of affairs in India regarding the COVID-19 plague, which has seen the country report high number of infections and deaths daily.

1. The Standard

The daily focused on the 2022 succession politics, but this time looking at the candidates hopeful of occupying the deputy president's office.

With the presidential candidates stepping up for the race, those wishing to deputise them are equally coming out as they draw out strategies to woo the top seat's candidates.

The top contenders are seemingly lost for choices as their top cronies line up to seek the deputy's post.

Tanga Tanga loyalists from the Mt Kenya region have held meetings to deliberate how DP William Ruto would consider one of their own to run second after him.

Among those believed to be looking forward to deputising Ruto include former CS Mwangi Kiunjuri, Narc Kenya leader Martha Karua, Gatundu South MP Moses Kuria, and former Kiambu governor William Kabogo.

ODM leader Raila Odinga has attracted some top politicians, for instance, Kirinyaga governor Anne Waiguru, Kakamega's Wycliffe Oparnya, and those from the coast region are Mombasa's Hassan Joho and Amason Kingi of Kilifi.

2. Daily Nation

Daily Nation reported on the COVID-19 situation in India.

The daily reported that hospitals in New Delhi had begun getting overwhelmed as the cases run on a high surge as the number of deaths shocked the world.

In the past 24 hours, fresh 349,691 cases were reported, with 2,767 patients succumbing to the virus within the same timeline.

However, the government is blamed for failing to prepare ahead of the surge despite early indications.

Prime Minister Narendra Modi termed the pandemic as a storm that had shaken the country, calling on Indians to get vaccinated and shun the rumours spreading regarding the dreaded disease.

Some states and territories within the country, including Delhi, have already effected movement restrictions to reduce the rate of infections.

3. People Daily

The publication also looked at the 2022 politics, the key focus being on the candidates looking forward to taking the deputy president position.

With the Mt Kenya region seemingly being the most sought after by the contenders, the region's politicians are keen on maintaining their presence in the presidency, having reigned in four terms since independence.

The Tanga Tanga leaders from the region have declared their support for DP Ruto. They, however, want Ruto to pick one of their own as his running mate in the coming vote.

Raila Odinga, on his part, also has a host of politicians who have already shown interest in walking with him to state house if he throws his hat in the ring.

4. The Star

The daily told its readers about Busia senator Amos Wako, who reflected on his life after being put down by COVID-19.

After the experience, Wako said he realised that wealth and power do not matter.

He said he knew he would not come out of the hospital and had already prepared for the life after death.

Wako had three spiritual books by his side, which he kept reading as he prepared for the "afterlife".

After winning against the virus, Wako, a former Attorney General, said he had reviewed how he had looked at life, adding he had changed his perspective.

“Having gone through it and been close to death, anybody who’s gone through this disease, their perspective changes," he said.

He says he changed his priorities and no longer looks at life the same way.

5. Taifa Leo

The Swahili publication featured a COVID-19 survivor who came out with great lessons to share.

Meru governor Kiraitu Murungi shared his experience with COVID-19, which he said changed his view of life.

Kiraitu tested positive for COVID-19 in mid-March 2021, and during those gruelling days that he was fighting the virus, he saw the proverbial light and realised there was more to life than politics.

During his recovery, the seclusion from the world brought to the fore the unnecessary sacrifices he had made due to politics and how much his career had taken from him.

The governor said politics had robbed his freedom of thought, life, and voice, and because of his obsession with winning, he had advocated for agendas that were not genuine to him.

However, with his recuperation comes a new dawn, and he now will focus on the things that actually matter in his life.
