Abdul Juma Speaks on Paternity Saga, Says Diamond Platnumz's Mother Confused Son

Post

- Abdul Juma has spoken out for the first time since his ex-wife claimed Diamond is not his son

- Abdul said the claims hurt him, and he stayed indoors for two months to avoid ridicule

- The old man said Diamond reached out to him on Eid after sending him money

Months after his ex-wife Sanura alleged that he is not Diamond Platnumz's father, Abdul Juma has broken silence on the matter.

Abdul said he does not blame Diamond for the saga and instead laid the blame squarely on the singer's mother.

According to Abdul, it beats logic that all of Diamond's documents bear his name, yet Sanura said he is not the singer’s father.

He further accused Sanura of confusing Diamond by saying one Nyange sired him.

"Diamond has not spoken because his mother confused him with these claims. I had never heard of Mzee Nyange till Sanura came out with the claims, and that is why I told Diamond to stop using my name,” he told Global Publishers.

Stress, forgiveness

Abdul said that he has let go of the anger and gave Diamond the green light to continue using his name.

However, amid the paternity drama, Abdul said that he locked himself indoors for two months.

“I was hurt when Diamond rejected me. I was indoors for two months. People only forgot about the saga when Magufuli died,” he let on.

Money

Abdul further said that Diamond reached out to him after sending him money for Eid celebrations.

“On Eid, I was asleep when I received some money on my phone. I received a call, and it was Diamond speaking. He identified himself as my son. I called Sheikhs and neighbours to my house, and we held prayers and had a meal together,” he said.

He said that Diamond’s half-sister Queen Darlin also sent him money on the same day.

Reliving the paternity drama, Abdul said he does not know what triggered Sanura to say Diamond is not his son.

“Before the incident, we were on good terms, and her current husband Shamte even told me to go see my grandchildren. It hurts that my co-parent is living a good life courtesy of the children I helped care for,” he said.

However, Abdul said he holds no grudge against Sanura and wished her the best in her marriage.

Abdul also pointed out he would not dictate to his son who to marry.

“I will not tell Diamond who to marry. I will have no issue with whoever he chooses. He has been singing he would settle down, but we are yet to see that,” he said.
