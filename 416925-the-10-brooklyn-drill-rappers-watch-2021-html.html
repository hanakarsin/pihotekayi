The 10 best Brooklyn drill rappers you should watch in 2021

July 17, 2024
Post

Music is a universal language. An artist can communicate with fans all over the world through rhymes alone. Brooklyn drill rappers have not only mastered this art but are also highly gifted, as seen in their music.

Every artist has reinvented their style to ensure that fans are always entertained and remain connected for more music. This has been the case for Brooklyn drill rappers who are conquering the music industry by day.

10 best Brooklyn drill rappers

The death of the fast-rising rapper Pop Smoke saw the rise of more drill rappers. The Brooklyn drill sound gained more fans and currently takes pride in having huge names representing this wave. Here is a list of the top 10 best Brooklyn drill rappers you should watch out for:

1. Fivio Foreign

In recent times, Fivio Foreign is quickly becoming one of the biggest names in the Brooklyn drill scene. With hit tracks like Ay, Ay, Ay, his popularity continues to go up by the day.

In addition, some of his tracks, such a Big Drip, Wetty, and one featuring Drake's recent jam Demons, are the bomb. In April 2020, the singer released his 800 BC mixtape, which was well-received by his fans and hip hop lovers in Brooklyn and beyond.

2. 22GZ

22GZ is another great name in Brooklyn drill world. He hails from the Flatbush region and is also part of the famous Sniper Gang. He has collaborated with the highly-acclaimed UK producer Ghosty, and this has seen him release hits after hits such as Pt. 2, Suburban, and No Questions.

He has gracefully earned the title of one of the fiercest rappers on the mic in Brooklyn, thanks to his unrelenting energy on his jams. In April 2020, the singer backed his reputation of being the best with the release of a genius mixtape titled, Growth and Development.

3. Sleepy Hollow

While most rappers from Brooklyn essentially borrow from the United Kingdom rap scene, Sleepy Hollow has chosen a different approach. His music is an infusion of Brooklyn drill with more traditional sounds of hip hop.

Sleepy rapper appeared on Lil TJay's hit sing, Wet em Up Pt 2 from the State of Emergency mixtape. The song was a great hit and featured other rappers such as Fivio Foreign and Pop Smoke. However, even with the collaborations, his releases have seen him build a strong foundation.

Some of his songs, Better Than Us and Panic Part 3, continue to be a hit to this day. In 2020, he released a banger, Deep End Freestyle, enabling him to reach a more mainstream audience.

4. Smoove L

Smoove L is a legendary artist with several years of experience in the music industry. Even though he has been in the industry for a few years, the increased attention in the Brooklyn Drill scene has seen his fame shoot to the rooftop.

Some of his hits, such as 2020 and Undefeated, have pushed him to the industry's forefront and enabled him to build a vital lane.

5. Sheff G

Sheff G stands as one of the most respected rappers in Brooklyn. In 2017, he released No Suburban, which UK producer AXL Beats produced. He has continued releasing great hits such as Panic and Disrespect. His most recent release of One and Only showcased a more laid-back style but remaining an icon.

6. Rah Swish

The list of the best Brooklyn drill rappers is incomplete without the mention of the talented Rah Swish. His music style infuses the UK sounds with that of Brooklyn drill. With several years of experience in music, he has honed his sound to become one of the leading names in the industry.

7. Curly Saw

The talented singer hails from Canarsie Brooklyn and is one of the best in the industry. His unique sound has made him stand out from the rest of Brooklyn drill rappers. The singer has collaborated with renowned UK producers such as Trap House Mob to release great hits.

Even though he has made hit jams to his name, he gained a broad fan base in 2020. This was after Glocky SZN, featuring talented Brooklyn drill rappers Fivio Foreign and Rah Swish.

8. Rowdy Rebel

Rowdy Rebel is a talented drill rapper from Brooklyn and an original member of the famous Bobby Shmurda-led hip-hop collective GS9. Initially, he gained a considerable following for being in prison, but he is now famous for all the right reasons.

9. CJ

CJ is a talented white Brooklyn drill rapper whose fame instantly shot to the sky thanks to TikTok. His single track, Whoopty, took an explosive on this platform thanks to the millions of TikTokers lip-syncing and dancing to this tune.

Initially, his music style was melodic rap but has now shifted to dabbling drill. The shift has turned out to be the perfect call as it has seen him become a star in Brooklyn drill rap. Some of his other releases include Exposing Me, Hit Up and Real One.

10. Bobby Shmurda

Who started Brooklyn drill rap? The talented Bobby Shmurda takes great pride in being one of the Brooklyn drill rap pioneers. The rapper also doubles up as a songwriter. He gained global recognition in 2014 after the release of Hot Nigga, which peaked at number 6 on the Billboard Hot 100.
