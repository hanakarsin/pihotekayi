Ali Kiba Responds to Queries on Diamond's New Escalades: &quot;I Don't Like Cars&quot;

Tanzanian singer Diamond Platnumz's perceived music rival Ali Kiba has spoken out for the first time about the Wasafi Classic Baby (WCB) co-founder acquiring two Cadillac Escalades.

Speaking to the press on Wednesday June 30, 2021, the Macmuga singer seemed uncomfortable speaking about Diamond.

The first question posed to Kiba was about his silence following Diamond’s nomination to the BET Awards under the Best International Act category.

Kiba openly expressed his displeasure with the line of questioning.

“I would like to ask you about Diamond. He was nominated for the BET Awards, and Tanzanians expected that you would post about it and support him,” a journalist told Kiba.

The moment the scribe mentioned Diamond’s name, Kiba touched his shades, seemingly irritated.

“I don’t like that question,” he responded.

The relentless journalist then followed up with a question about Diamond’s new Sports Utility Vehicles (SUVs), telling Kiba he has not publicly shown his love for high-end vehicles.

“You have not publicly shown that you like expensive cars,” the scribe said to Kiba. “That is true. I don't like cars,” a smiling Kiba responded before walking away, leaving behind an amused crowd.

Diamond's Escalades

The question on Diamond's cars comes a week after the singer's mother unveiled a second Escalade he purchased. Diamond first acquired a KSh 32 million Cadillac Escalade Concept One Curve weeks earlier.

Diamond's mother posted videos of the second SUV being delivered to his mansion while Rayvanny’s manager Don Fumbwe shared a video of the odometer, proving the vehicle is brand new.

Rivalry

Kiba and Diamond's rivalry seemed to boil to the surface in May when the former bashed the Kamata singer for complaining over a fake Forbes list.

"Not a single one of the people you lie to yourself are your peers have announced they have money on Facebook. So there’s no need to complain. If you tell us you have money, we will believe you,” Kiba wrote.
