Kenyan songstress Nyota Ndogo has expressed her joy after a member of a chama she started finally started building a house.

Via an Instagram post on July 13, 2021, the Watu Na Viatu hitmaker said that she started the chama four years ago through social media.

According to the singer, she started the outfit with the sole aim of helping women build houses.

“Four years ago, I started a chama via social media with 20 woman. I collected them from Instagram and Facebook. The chama aimed to help each woman build their own house,” she wrote.

A chama is an informal cooperative society or a micro-savings group, They are synonymous with table banking and merry-go-rounds.

Nyota Ndogo admitted to facing unprecedented challenges writing:

“The journey had not been as easy as I had imagined, but since I started the chama, I accepted the ups and downs and strived to see the project through.”

The musician expounded on the chama, saying they gave monthly contributions of KSh 5,000, which enabled them to purchase land.

“Each one of us used to give KSh 5,000 every month for six months, and we acquired a plot and the title deed,” she said.

COVID-19 halts construction

Before they started construction in 2020, the pandemic hit, and some of the chama members lost their jobs.

A year later, one of the chama members started building her house, and Nyota Ndogo celebrated her by writing:

“I am happy to inform you that one of us started building her house today. I am so proud to see the fruition of what I started. Even If I die, I will be remembered.”

Nyota Ndogo further urged netizens to use the internet constructively.

“The internet connects us, and if we are keen, we will build each other. If we are not, we will just trade insults,” she concluded.
