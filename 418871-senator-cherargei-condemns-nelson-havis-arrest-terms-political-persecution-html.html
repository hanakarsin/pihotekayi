Nandi senator Kiprotich Cherargei has accused the State of engineering a plan to curtail the powers of the Law Society of Kenya (LSK).

The legislator claimed that some top state officials were not happy with the society’s proactivity in championing the upholding and implementation of the constitution.

The senator was reacting to the arrest of the LSK president Nelson Havi over the alleged assault of the society’s suspended CEO, Mercy Wambua.

Cherargei said the arrest and querying of Havi were not in good faith but politically motivated, owing to the LSK president’s position on the controversial Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) constitution amendment plan.

“Nelson Havi is being politically persecuted for opposing BBI in court plus his reluctance to be a State gatekeeper,” the senator told TUKO.co.ke.

He further called on all advocates in the country to religiously defend the independence of the society.

“All the advocates and LSK in its entirety must reject this State arm twisting lest and remote controlling lest what befell Kenya National Union of Teachers befalls us. Be on the alert,” said Cherargei who is also an Advocate of the High Court.

The leadership row between Havi and Wambua at LSK escalated during a boardroom meeting, after which the CEO claimed she was physically assaulted.

Havi, who was released from Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) headquarters on Tuesday, July 13, on a KSh 10,000 bail, denied assaulting Wambua, saying he only closed her laptop and ordered her to leave the meeting.

Wambua had recorded a police statement alleging she had been assaulted during the meeting held at the society’s Gatanga Road-based offices.

In her statement, the suspended CEO said Havi tried to push her from the boardroom where she was having a meeting because he had another meeting scheduled to take place in the same venue.

According to Wambua’s lawyer, Steve Ogolla, his client dodged a slap from Havi, forcing her out of the boardroom, then slammed her laptop and crashed her fingers.

Mercy Wambua's allegations

Wambua alleged the LSK president physically and verbally assaulted her at their Gatanga road-based offices during a meeting on Monday, July 12.

In a case reported at Muthangari Police Station, Wambua stated that Havi injured her right arm and finger during the scuffle.

The embattled CEO said she attended the meeting as a bonafide member, only to be met with shouts and calls asking her to leave.

She accused the LSK boss of forcing her out while at the same time assaulting her.

Wambua claimed that the LSK boss convened a meeting and invited members who support his leadership. She stated that while other members attended physically, a section of them joined virtually.
