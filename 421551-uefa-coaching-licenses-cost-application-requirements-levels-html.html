UEFA coaching licenses: cost, application, requirements, levels

July 05, 2024
Post

Before becoming an elite coach in Europe, the first step is to obtain your coaching badges. That includes going for any of the available UEFA coaching licenses. As a UEFA-qualified coach, you will have the opportunity to manage a team from the grassroots community to the top of international football. The purpose of the licenses is to improve coaching standards while ensuring that European football is at the forefront of coach education excellence.

UEFA has several coaching licenses available for all interested individuals. The federation endorses licenses A, B, C, and Pro and has specialist qualifications at Youth B, Elite Youth A, Goalkeeper A, Goalkeeper B, and Futsal B levels.

Ideally, you can get a license from a member association, a body responsible for governing football in its respective country. The association must meet UEFA criteria and standards to qualify. Here is what you need to know about UEFA coaching licenses, including application, requirements, levels, and cost.

What are the certified UEFA coaching licenses?

A UEFA coaching license is also known as a coaching diploma. It is a qualification awarded to any individual who has completed a coaching course. Once you get a UEFA coaching license, it will be valid for three years until you renew it. UEFA has four mainstream qualifications that include:

The UEFA C Diploma

The UEFA C Diploma is the newest qualification offered by UEFA, aimed at grassroots coaches. It covers essential topics, such as child safeguarding, basic first aid, and the importance of promoting a healthy, active lifestyle. Details of the course vary between member associations.

The course length for UEFA C Diploma is a minimum of 60 hours of education, with 50 hours spent studying guided interactive content and ten hours of work experience. A person with the UEFA C License can work as a coach at the grassroots levels and create opportunities for other individuals of different abilities, backgrounds, and ages to play the game.

The UEFA B Diploma

The UEFA B Diploma is a course that teaches an individual age and ability-specific coaching techniques. Its function is to ensure that coaches can develop players at the youth level through the senior amateur level. The course involves managing all aspects of improving individual and team performance.

It also involves working with the support staff, evaluation, analysis, and providing feedback on match and training performances. The course length for the UEFA B license courses is a minimum of 120 hours, with 95 hours spent studying guided interactive content and 25 hours on work experience at a club.

After getting the UEFA B license, you will coach grassroots and elite football at the youth and senior levels. Your work will also involve catering to the needs of all players.

The UEFA A Diploma

The UEFA A Diploma is UEFA's second-highest coaching qualification. It gives an individual a clear understanding of player and team development when coaching at the top of the amateur level. The entire course provides a participant with insight into technical policy and deals with external factors affecting a team's performance.

One also gets to learn more about accountability to club management. The course involves both theoretical and practical work. In other words, one will learn on the training pitch how to design game-oriented sessions, focus on opponents, pressure, intensity, prepare a detailed game plan, and create a winning mentality in a team.

The course duration is 180 hours, with 140 hours spent studying guided interactive content and 40 hours on work experience at a club. It will take at least six months to complete the UEFA A diploma.

What level can you coach with UEFA A Licence?

The UEFA A license allows you to work predominantly as the head coach of a top amateur team. In other cases, you can use it to work as a coaching staff member at a professional level. In addition, you will have a central role in developing and applying technical policy at a football club.

How much does UEFA A Licence cost?

The UEFA coaching license cost varies from one UEFA member country to another. For example, in a 2013 article by ESPN, it costs a maximum of £5,820 or $8,085 in current currency to get the UEFA A license in England. In Germany, the cost is €530 or $620, and €1,200 or $1,458 in Spain.

The cost is also different in other member countries. For example, in Holland and Denmark, one will have to pay €9,700 or $11,498. Romania charges €700 or $829, and Estonia costs €900 or $1,066.

The UEFA Pro Diploma

The UEFA Pro Diploma is the highest coaching qualification in European football. It is a course that gives a participant a clear understanding of player and team development. One also gets to learn more about the role of a head coach at a professional level.

It helps coaches develop and apply technical policies and philosophies and create a winning team and high-performance culture. The UEFA Pro Diploma requires a minimum of 360 hours to learn, with 140 hours spent studying theory and practical units off the pitch and 220 hours on work experience on the field.

A participant has to complete the course in one season, including study visits. After obtaining the UEFA Pro License, you can work as the head coach of a professional club by managing the first team or becoming a professional coach member at a professional level.

The UEFA Pro license cost also varies from one country to another. For example, a person will have to part ways with £9,890 or $13,740 to complete the course in England. The programme duration is 18 months. In Ireland, it costs €7,550 or $8,952.

How do you enrol for a UEFA coaching license?

The requirements will differ depending on the course and your country of origin. For example:

  • You will have to contact your national association for the latest course information, availability, and eligibility for UEFA C Diploma.
  • Before enrolling for UEFA B Diploma, you must have at least six months of coaching experience and a valid UEFA C license.
  • You require a valid UEFA B Diploma and at least one year of experience coaching a football team after graduating with the B diploma to enrol for a UEFA A Diploma.
  • You require a valid UEFA A license to enrol for the UEFA Pro Diploma. Furthermore, you must have completed at least one year of coaching after graduating with the A license. Your position must be a head coach at an elite youth or senior amateur level or an assistant coach in a professional football team.

Can you obtain a UEFA coaching license in the USA?

You cannot obtain a UEFA coaching license in the USA. The permit applies to football associations that are members of UEFA. UEFA does not have coaching courses online. If you are in the USA and want to obtain the UEFA coaching license, you will have to move to the member country. Assuming you want to do it in English, you will have to register with the Football Association of England, Wales, Ireland, or Scotland.

UEFA coaching licenses come in various categories, including UEFA C, B, A, and Pro. The UEFA Pro license is the highest coaching qualification one can obtain. But it requires a person to have received licenses C, B, and A to enrol.
