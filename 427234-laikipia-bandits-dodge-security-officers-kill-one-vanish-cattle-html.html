Laikipia: Bandits Dodge Security Officers, Kill One and Vanish with Cattle

One person has been killed and tens of cattle stolen by bandits in the latest attack in the Wangwashi area within Ol Moran in Laikipia county.

The area is still under security operation with a 30-day dusk to dawn curfew in effect.

Locals dissatisfied with the security operation

But the locals have expressed dissatisfaction with the conduct of the operation.

NTV Kenya reports that two weeks of calm in Wangwashi have been shattered by the death of a 64-year-old man killed by bandits as he looked after cows on Wednesday, September 22.

Eyewitnesses said the attack happened a kilometre away from where the military was digging a trench separating Ol Moran and the Laikipia Nature Conservancy.

''We were feeding our cattle before we were attacked by the bandits when we were heading to the waterhole before returning home. The bandits, unfortunately, killed the old man identified as Maina,'' said Samuel Ruchuiri, a survivor.

The attackers are said to have led the stolen livestock into the vast Laikipia Nature Conservancy.

Security operation intensified

In this exact location, security officers have intensified the security operation to rid the troubled area of gun-wielding pastoralists.

''The bandits were not in a hurry. They had the confidence that they could succeed in terrorising us. They drove away over 30 cattle. We don't feel like we are being protected as we would want it to be,'' Ruchuiri said.

Locals said that the situation had made it impossible for students to report back to school for fear of being attacked.

''For how long should we help support from the government? They should come here and address this situation before it completely gets out of hand because our school-going children are now at home,'' said Susan Njeri, a resident.

Passage of livestock

On the morning of the attack, it is believed that a delegation of leaders from the neighbouring Isiolo county sought an audience with the security teams asking for the safe passage of their livestock a fortnight after the government's 48-hour notice expired.

Rift Valley Regional Coordinator George Natembeya while leading an operation at the Laikipia Nature Conservancy on Tuesday, September 7, said the bandits own guns that cannot be matched by those used by the Kenyan police officers.

He said that some of the weapons, like the M16 guns, are owned by foreign troops who conduct paramilitary training in the region.
